#### <font color="red"><ins>Coquelet</ins></font>

Coquelet is an MFSK system, comparable to Piccolo. Uses the ITA-2 character set. The two main modes are Coquelet-8 and Coquelet-13.

Coquelet was developed in the early 1960s by ACEC in Belgium for use by the police and military. It was extended to French security and customs use, as well as to the Algerian Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MFA) and Customs use (mainly Coquelet-8 and Coquelet-80). Algeria was one of the last users of Coquelet, when they switched to newer modes they had tested, such as Racal MSM1250 "SkyFax" modems and modified PACTOR systems.

- Coquelet-8: 8-tone 300 Hz wide MFSK. Coquelet-8 has baud rates of 13.33 Bd, 20 Bd and 26.67 Bd.
- Coquelet-13: 13-tone 500 Hz wide MFSK. Coquelet-13 has baud rates of 13.33 Bd and 20 Bd.
- Coquelet-80: Variant of Coquelet-8. Coquelet-80 adds FEC to the Coquelet-8 waveform. Includes sub-variants Coquelet-80S and Coquelet-82S. The main difference is that Coquelet-82S can be used in both sidebands and uses extended handshaking and synchronisation sequences. Coquelet-80 borrows the same bandwidth and baud rates from Coquelet-8.
